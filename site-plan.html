Overview

Purpose

Our purpose on this site is to allow adventurers and outdoor enthusiasts to browse our huge selection of river gear. We have everything you need from kayaks and river rafts to life jackets and customizable apparel! We also include a page that will provide you with all the river locations in the United States and it will take you to their websites to get all the information you need to start your river adventure! Please join us so we can make your river trip one for the books!

Are you a river rafting company? Need some equipment? Because our stuff comes in bulk too! We provide top end tubes and rafts at an economic price! Check them out!

Audience

Our audience includes the young and old who love the outdoors. Our product is geared towards new adventurers who are itching to get into river rafting. Many people don’t know where to start! We provide everything they need to get started. We also provide for those who are already professionals. Gear doesn’t last forever, so we are here to provide new oars when they get lost on the water, or new kayaks for the kids! We are also targeting new companies that are starting their business on a river, they will need tubes for their customers. We also provide tubes for current companies that need their equipment replaced when their life jackets get worn out and when their tubes pop. This will all be accessed on any desktop, laptop or mobile device.

Typography

Heading Font: 'Dosis', sans-serif

I chose this font because I was looking for something that looked clean and professional, but not too boring to read.

Paragraph Font: 'Balsamiq Sans', cursive

To follow the professional header, I wanted something that told the readers that we provide fun experiences!

Normal paragraph example

The Site Map of a site is just like it sounds…it is a map of the pages in a site and how they are related and linked together. From the map above we can see that we will eventually have the Home page and 2 sub or child pages.

The lines that connect them all together indicate that each page should be accessible from any other page, it is essentially showing us the global navigation for the site.

Wireframes

Wireframes are like blueprints for making webpages. They should show the major sections of content that will be on the page and the relative locations of each element. In the wireframe below you can see there will be 6 sections to our page:

  1. At the top we have a section with the logo (the box with the mountain means an image) and the navigation bar.
  2. Then there is a banner image that stretches all the way across the screen.
  3. Next we have some text and an image
  4. ...followed by another row made up of an image and some text.
  5. Then one more section of text with no image.
  6. Lastly, a footer containing a copyright/name line and 3 social media icons.
